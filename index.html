1 | What's it all about?

About

Colab is the collaboratory for Design and Creative Technologies at the Auckland University of Technology (AUT), New Zealand.
Its aim is to inspire creative research and collaboration between lecturers and students. Colab Conference is an opportunity for lecturers to expand their practice and showcase their work and expertise, to a group of interested students involved in a broad range of similar disciplines.
We hope to encourage the BCT culture of working together by hearing real world ideas and research that will ultimately affect the way Creative Technologies evolves in the future. Participants will also be invited to debate relevant student chosen questions designed to explore the undefinable and the future of Creative Technologies in a rapidly changing society.

2 | What we need?

Call for papers

theme

The theme for this conference, “Plato’s Colab", explores creative technology as a utopian ideology. Lecturers are invited to submit papers either on this theme or on any topic you consider relevant, including but not limited to entrepreneurship, creative management, interactive art, design thinking, film, virtual reality and the future of Creative Technology, in the context of the platonic utopia.

"Like Plato’s Republic and Laws presented ideals of a utopian society, Creative Technology presents itself as a mode of elevating creativity, thinking and practice past the confines of traditional disciplinary frameworks, into a space that offers true and unbounded innovative potential. This conference theme explores the utopian ideals of creative technology and how work being done in the field of creative technology reflects, reinforces and challenges these ideals."

3 | When, What, How?

Details

Colab Conference 2016 will be held on RED Friday, 29 July 2016, in the second week of semester 2. New papers are encouraged and previously published papers, if reworked and/or relevant, will be considered. Long or short papers should be between 2 and 5 pages in length. Papers will be assessed by a panel of students and selected based on quality and relevance. Submissions must conform to the standards specified in the attached template. Special consideration will be given to works of art or installations if applicable.

publication
